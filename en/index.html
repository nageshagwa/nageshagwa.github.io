How the counts are calculated

The tool first splits text into lines and paragraphs by line breaks, then scans character by character: anything in the CJK ideograph, kana or Hangul ranges counts as one Chinese character, while each whitespace-delimited run elsewhere counts as one Western word. This matches the "Chinese characters and Korean words" plus "English words" logic in WPS and Microsoft Word word count, so mixed text counts correctly too.

What gets counted

Characters (with spaces) counts every character including spaces and punctuation; characters (no spaces) drops all whitespace; Chinese characters counts only ideographs and kana/Hangul; paragraphs are separated by blank lines. Invisible characters such as CR/LF and tabs affect the byte count but are not counted as words by default.

Frequently asked questions

Does this word counter upload my text?
No. All counting and conversion run locally in your browser with JavaScript; your text is never sent to any server and is cleared when you close the page.
How are Chinese characters counted?
Each character in the CJK ideograph, extension, kana and Hangul ranges counts as one Chinese character, matching the "Chinese characters and Korean words" measure in WPS and Word.
What is the difference between words and characters?
Characters counts every character including spaces and punctuation; words equals Chinese characters plus Western words, closer to the word count used for essays or paid writing.
Does it match WPS and Word word count?
For pure Chinese or pure English text it usually matches; with mixed text, many symbols or full-width characters there may be a small difference, because each tool classifies punctuation and spaces slightly differently.
Can it count English words?
Yes. After removing CJK characters, each whitespace-delimited run is a Western word, which is handy for word limits on English essays and abstracts.
What is the UTF-8 byte count for?
Database fields, forms and APIs often limit length by bytes. A Latin letter takes 1 byte and a Chinese character usually takes 3, so the byte count helps you avoid being truncated.
